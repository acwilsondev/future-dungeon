# Future Dungeon: Exotic Build Paths & Galactic Fates

In the *Future Dungeon* system, character progression is defined by how the player interacts with the **[Voidstar](lore.md)**—the octahedral source of power at the center of the celestial conflict. While the **Fighter** provides a reliable physical foundation of **Iron**-tier equipment and high attributes, these paths represent radical shifts in how a player engages with the ASCII grid, the factions within it, and the ultimate fate of the galaxy.

## Default Ending

The Ending: Galactic Stasis

By recovering the [Voidstar](lore.md) without a definitive alignment, you effectively act as a cosmic circuit breaker rather than a conductor. You have removed the Biotic Eye, but failed to replace it with a new "God" or a "Market Leader."

1. The Power Stalemate
    The Factions: The Solari and Nihil remain locked in their "As Above, So Below" conflict. Without a mandated leader, they continue their localized skirmishes, unable to gain a decisive advantage over one another.
    The "Fray": The phenomenon previously known as the "glitch" (now called the Fray) persists as a fundamental part of existence, neither expanding into a total collapse nor being cured by Aetheric harmony.
2. Industrial Fragmentation
    The Iron Tier: While the Iron Contra ending represents a monopoly on energy, the Status Quo ending sees the [Voidstar](lore.md)'s power fragmented.
    Outcome: Small industrial sectors utilize fragments of the star's energy to keep the lights on, but the "Galactic Capitalism" era is held at bay by the lingering spiritual interference of the player’s unrefined mana.
3. Contained Biomass
    The Emerald Sector: Without a Hivemind to unify them, the Biomass remains a dangerous environmental hazard rather than an extinction-level event.
    Outcome: The "Great Hive" continues to exist in the dark corners of the galaxy, but it is successfully kept at bay by Iron-tier barricades and fire.

## Special Endings

### I. The Aligned Specialists (Celestial)

By dedicating the full 5-pip mana limit to one side of the "As Above, So Below" spectrum, the player becomes a conduit for absolute celestial power.

#### 1. The Solari (Nova Paragon)

* **Vibe:** High-tech paladins and solar monks powered by the blinding light of a newborn star.
* **Gameplay:** Focuses on **Radiance** (illuminating the map and burning enemies), **Shielding** (HP buffers), and **Fortify** (additive armor/health bonuses).
* **The Fate:** Upon acquiring the [Voidstar](lore.md), the Solari launch a jihad on nonbelievers to establish a galactic theocracy where privacy and darkness cease to exist.

### 2. The Nihil (Void Sovereign)

* **Vibe:** Mystic assassins and warlock-like figures utilizing the crushing weight of black holes.
* **Gameplay:** Focuses on **Entropy** (permanent armor/weapon degradation), **Gravity** (pulling entities toward a center point), and **Gloom** (severely limiting enemy vision).
* **The Fate:** The Nihil accelerate the end of the universe, setting in motion a 1,000-year plan to darken all stars and grant the galaxy the "Gift of Silence."

### II. Aetheric Equilibrium (The Transcendent Path)

This path utilizes the "Glitch" logic of the Aetheric creator-race by maintaining a delicate balance between the two celestial forces.

* **Requirements:** Maintain exactly 2 Solari and 2 Nihil mana pips (the "Secret" middle path).
* **Gameplay:** Unlocks **Cyan (Aetheric)** "Grid Violation" tools such as **Phase** (walking through walls), **Blink** (instant teleportation), and **Flicker** (desyncing from the turn to ignore damage).
* **The Fate:** By finding perfect alignment and recovering the [Voidstar](lore.md), the player transcends the grid and ascends to the Aetheric plane. The Aetheric rebuild the galaxy in a harmonious, non-Euclidean image.

### III. The Iron Contra (The Industrialist)

The path of "Pure Grit," rejecting both magical and biological evolution in favor of maximizing mundane industrial technology and physical reliability.

* **Requirements:** Stay grounded in **Iron**-tier logic, ignoring all Mana and Mutations.
* **Gameplay:** Focuses on **Hard Collisions** and tactical tools like **Impact** (massive knockback), **Barricade** (deployable walls), and **Obscure** (smoke screens). Naturally **Hardened** against Energy Drains and EMPs.
* **The Fate:** The player treats the [Voidstar](lore.md) as an infinite source of power, entering a new era of galactic capitalism with a strict monopoly on energy and a galaxy turned into one efficient, soulless factory.

### IV. The Biomass (The "Becoming")

By rejecting all mana, the player remains "spiritually silent," allowing them to commune with and eventually become the hive.

* **Requirements:** Maintain 0/0 Mana and refuse all celestial meditation.
* **Gameplay:** Permanent **Mutations** replace standard **Iron** equipment slots (e.g., **Chitinous Scythe** replacing the Main Hand weapon). Emerald entities (Biomass) treat the player as neutral.
* **The Fate:** By acquiring the [Voidstar](lore.md) and unifying with the Biomass, the player becomes the Hivemind. The Biomass takes over the galaxy, folding every sentient being into a single, breathing organism.

### V. The Catalyst: The Fall of the Biotic Eye

The **Biotic Eye**—a super-entity merger of Aetheric and Biomass forces—guards the [Voidstar](lore.md) at the bottom of the dungeon. Its death acts as the final systemic collapse, allowing the player's specific build philosophy to flood the vacuum it leaves behind and trigger the galaxy's final fate.
